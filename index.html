About Organisation

(Drone Pilot Training – Small Category )

A certified RPTO under rule 39 of the Drone Rules 2021, Approved by Director General of Civil Aviation (DGCA), New Delhi - 110003

The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) in India is the regulatory body in the field of Civil Aviation (Aircrafts and Drones) primarily dealing with safety issues. DGCA has made Drone pilot training mandatory for any drone above 250 gm in India. Drone pilot training is conducted for a minimum of five days which includes theory, simulator, and flight training. The training is provided to make the pilot efficient enough to fly the drone within the Drone Rules, 2021 published by the Ministry of Civil Aviation, India. The Remote Pilot Certificate (RPC) is provided as the license to operate drones till 25kg. The drones are used for various applications in fields like Aerial photography, goods delivery, disaster management, search and rescue operations, geographic mapping, building safety inspections, crop monitoring and spraying, etc.

Eligibility

  • Age Range: 18 to 65 years
  • Educational Requirement: Successful completion of the 10th-grade examination
  • Identification: An Aadhar Card linked with a valid mobile number is required.
  • Mandatory Travel Document: Possession of a valid passport is mandatory for international training and job placement opportunities.

Course Outcomes

    At the end of the course students are able to:
  • Understand how to control a drone and its movements.
  • Identify the key parts of a drone and explain their functions.
  • Recognize and name common drone components.
  • Be familiar with emergency procedures and fail-safe mechanisms.
  • Explain different drone maintenance practices and follow standard operating procedures (SOP) for safe drone operation.
